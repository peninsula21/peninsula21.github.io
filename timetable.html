Our Staffing Model

A small, intentional school means every child is known deeply. Our staffing structure is designed to make genuinely personalised learning possible every single day.

1

VIT-Registered Lead Teacher

A single qualified teacher leads learning across all year levels Foundation to Year 6, holding full responsibility for curriculum delivery, planning and reporting against Victorian Curriculum F–10 achievement standards.

3

Education Support Staff

Three dedicated education support staff work alongside the teacher across all composite classes, providing hands-on support during personalised learning blocks and afternoon STEM sessions.

6:1

Student–to–Staff Ratio

With 21 students and 4 staff members on the floor each day, we maintain an exceptional 6:1 ratio — far exceeding standard primary benchmarks and enabling truly responsive, individualised attention.

Personalised Learning at Peninsula21

Our composite class structure is a feature, not a constraint. It mirrors the real world and creates a rich multi-age learning environment where older students lead and younger students aspire.

How personalisation works

Each student has a learning profile that is built with their family and updated each term. The lead teacher uses Victorian Curriculum achievement levels — not year levels — to plan targeted learning programs for every child.

  • Individual learning goals set with families each term
  • Curriculum delivered at the student's actual achievement level
  • Education support staff circulate to provide 1:1 and small-group support
  • Formative assessment embedded daily — not just at report time
  • Digital portfolio tracks each student's learning journey

The composite class advantage

Multi-age groupings are well-supported by research and are a natural fit for a small school with big ambitions. Peninsula21's three composite classes — Prep/1, Year 2/3, and Year 4/5 — each have roughly 7 students with at least one dedicated support staff member at all times.

  • Older students reinforce their own learning by supporting peers
  • Younger students are exposed to stretch thinking and problem-solving
  • Collaborative STEM and digital projects span year levels naturally
  • Strong relationships built across 2-year cycles with the same teacher
  • Each class group has approximately 7 students — an intimate learning community
