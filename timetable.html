Our Staffing Model

A small, intentional school means every child is known deeply. Our staffing structure is designed to make genuinely personalised learning possible every single day.

1

VIT-Registered Lead Teacher

A single qualified teacher leads learning across all year levels Foundation to Year 6, holding full responsibility for curriculum delivery, planning and reporting against Victorian Curriculum F–10 achievement standards.

2

Education Support Staff

Two dedicated education support staff work alongside the teacher every day, each supporting a class group during STEM and digital literacy sessions and circulating during morning KLA blocks for targeted 1:1 support.

7:1

Student–to–Staff Ratio

With 21 students and 3 staff on the floor each day (1 teacher + 2 ES), we maintain a 7:1 ratio — well below typical Victorian primary class sizes and the foundation of genuinely personalised learning.

Curriculum compliance: All 8 Key Learning Areas of the Victorian Curriculum F–10 Version 2.0 are substantially addressed each week in line with VRQA minimum standards. Languages is delivered by the VIT-registered teacher. A minimum of 25 minutes of systematic synthetic phonics is embedded in the daily literacy block (Prep–Year 2). All mandated time requirements including Health & Physical Education are met or exceeded. Subject to VRQA approval.

Personalised Learning at Peninsula21

Our composite class structure is a feature, not a constraint — a rich multi-age environment where older students lead and younger students aspire.

How personalisation works

Each student has a learning profile built with their family and updated each term. The lead teacher uses Victorian Curriculum achievement levels — not year levels — to plan targeted programs for every child.

  • Individual learning goals set with families each term
  • Curriculum delivered at the student's actual achievement level
  • Education support staff provide 1:1 and small-group support daily
  • Formative assessment embedded daily — not just at report time
  • Digital portfolio tracks each student's learning journey

The composite class advantage

Peninsula21's three composite classes — Prep/1, Year 2/3, and Year 4/5 — each have roughly 7 students, with the teacher and ES staff rotating across groups throughout the day.

  • Older students reinforce their own learning by supporting peers
  • Younger students are exposed to stretch thinking and problem-solving
  • STEM and digital projects span year levels naturally
  • Strong relationships built across 2-year cycles with the same teacher
  • Each class: approximately 7 students — an intimate learning community
